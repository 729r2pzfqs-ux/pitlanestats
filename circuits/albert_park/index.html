About Albert Park Grand Prix Circuit

The Albert Park Grand Prix Circuit winds through a public parkland surrounding a lake just south of Melbourne's city centre. A temporary street-style circuit laid out on park roads, it took over the Australian Grand Prix from Adelaide in 1996 and has been the race's home ever since, hosting the event for nearly three decades.

For most of that span Albert Park has served as the season opener, giving fans their first look at the new cars and pecking order each year. Damon Hill won the inaugural 1996 race for Williams, and the circuit has since produced victories for many of the sport's biggest names. The track was significantly reprofiled in 2022 to improve overtaking, with several corners widened and reshaped, and the modern era has delivered winners including Charles Leclerc, Carlos Sainz, Lando Norris and George Russell.

The race was absent in 2020 and 2021, cancelled amid the COVID-19 pandemic, but returned to its place near the front of the calendar. Set against Melbourne's skyline and the lake, it remains one of the most scenic and atmospheric venues in Formula 1.

Memorable Moments

  • 1996: Damon Hill won the first Australian Grand Prix held at Albert Park for Williams, opening a season in which he would go on to take the world title.
  • 2009: Jenson Button and the new Brawn GP team announced themselves with a dominant debut victory, the start of a fairy-tale championship run.
  • 2022: Charles Leclerc took a commanding win for Ferrari on the freshly revised layout, a statement drive that put him in early control of the championship.
  • 2025: Lando Norris won a chaotic rain-affected opener for McLaren, holding off a charging field on a slippery Melbourne surface.

Track Character

Albert Park blends the smoothness of a permanent circuit with the close walls of a street track, running on sealed park roads that are only resurfaced for the race weekend. The layout flows through a series of fast, sweeping corners broken up by heavy braking zones, with the post-2022 changes opening up several turns to encourage wheel-to-wheel racing. Overtaking comes mainly into the slower corners and on the long flat-out runs, where DRS gives a useful tow. The lakeside parkland setting, the unpredictable Melbourne weather and its role as the curtain-raiser of the season give the venue a distinctive energy.
