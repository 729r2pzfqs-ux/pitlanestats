About Jordan

Jordan Grand Prix, founded by the charismatic Irishman Eddie Jordan, joined Formula 1 in 1991 and immediately became one of the most popular and colourful teams on the grid. Operating on a modest budget against the giants of the sport, Jordan punched far above its weight and had a famous knack for spotting talent.

The team gave Michael Schumacher his Formula 1 debut at the 1991 Belgian Grand Prix before he was poached by Benetton. Jordan's bright livery and rock-and-roll image made it a fan favourite, and the team reached its peak in 1998 and 1999, winning races with Damon Hill and Heinz-Harald Frentzen and finishing third in the 1999 constructors' championship.

Rising costs eventually caught up with the independent squad, and it was sold in 2005. Through subsequent ownership changes the team continued under various names, forming part of the lineage that races on the modern grid.

Key Achievements

  • Four Grand Prix victories as an independent constructor
  • Gave Michael Schumacher his Formula 1 debut in 1991
  • Third in the 1999 Constructors' Championship
  • Famous for spotting and developing emerging driving talent
  • One of the most beloved underdog teams of the 1990s

Legacy & Identity

Jordan embodied the spirit of the plucky privateer, mixing serious racing with genuine charisma and fun. Eddie Jordan's eye for talent and gift for sponsorship kept a small team competitive against far richer rivals, and its golden-era wins remain among the most celebrated underdog stories in Formula 1.
