Most Wins in a Season

Highest Win Percentage in a Season

Win percentage levels the playing field between short and long calendars. Verstappen's 19-from-22 (86.4%) in 2023 is the highest ever, but the 1950s aces ran the closest: Ascari and Fangio each won three-quarters of the races they entered.

Podiums, Laps Led & Constructor Records

Verstappen's 2023 also brought 21 podiums and a barrier-breaking 1,003 laps led — the first four-figure total in history. For teams, Red Bull's 21 wins in 2023 is the most ever, while McLaren's 1988 remains the highest win rate by a constructor.

Season Records — FAQ

Who has the most wins in a single F1 season?

Max Verstappen, who won 19 of the 22 races in 2023. Michael Schumacher (2004) and Sebastian Vettel (2013) share the previous record of 13.

Who has the most poles in a single season?

Sebastian Vettel, with 15 pole positions in 2011, ahead of Nigel Mansell's 14 in 1992.

What is the highest win percentage in a season?

Max Verstappen's 86.4% (19 wins from 22 races) in 2023 is the highest by total. Alberto Ascari (1952) and Juan Manuel Fangio (1954) each won 75% of the races they entered.

What is the most points scored in a season?

Max Verstappen scored 575 points in 2023 — the most under the modern points system. Raw totals from earlier eras are not directly comparable due to different points systems.

Which constructor has the most wins in a season?

Red Bull won 21 of 22 races in 2023, the most ever. McLaren's 15 from 16 in 1988 (93.8%) remains a benchmark for win rate.
