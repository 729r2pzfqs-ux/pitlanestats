About PitlaneStats

Every lap. Every driver. Every season. Since the very first World Championship race at Silverstone in 1950, we've been cataloguing the numbers that tell the story of Formula 1.

What is PitlaneStats?

PitlaneStats is a comprehensive Formula 1 statistics database covering every season, driver, constructor, circuit, and race result since the inaugural 1950 World Championship. We built it for the fans, the analysts, and anyone who believes that the numbers behind the sport are just as thrilling as the racing itself.

Whether you're settling a debate about the greatest of all time, researching a driver's career trajectory, or simply exploring the rich history of the sport — PitlaneStats puts decades of data at your fingertips.

What You'll Find Here

Race Results

Complete results for every Grand Prix ever held — grids, finishes, fastest laps, and retirements going back over seven decades.

Driver Career Stats

Full career profiles for every driver who has entered a World Championship event — wins, poles, podiums, points, and championship positions.

Constructor Histories

Performance data for every constructor from the backmarkers to the dynasties — championship results, win counts, and season-by-season breakdowns.

Head-to-Head Comparisons

Compare any two drivers side by side — qualifying battles, race finishes, and career statistics laid out for direct comparison.

All-Time Records

Leaderboards and record tables across every statistical category — most wins, fastest laps, consecutive podiums, and hundreds more.

Circuit Profiles

Every circuit that has hosted a Grand Prix — race history, lap records, most successful drivers, and key statistics for each venue.

Our Data Source

PitlaneStats is powered by the Jolpica F1 API (Ergast compatible), one of the most comprehensive open sources of Formula 1 data available. The API provides structured, machine-readable access to results, standings, schedules, and more — spanning the entire history of the World Championship.

We process and present this data in a way that's fast, visual, and easy to explore — whether you're on a phone during a race weekend or deep-diving on desktop.
